Glossary

ADSL (Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line)
A high speed digital line. Like a traditional phone line, but it means you can get internet at broadband speed, instead of dial-up.


Anti-virus software
Protects your computer from harmful viruses and programs.


Driver
A file that makes a piece of equipment work with your computer (like a printer, modem, memory stick, camera or anything else you plug in).


GPRS (Options Packet Radio Service)
A way of getting the internet over the mobile phone network. It's also called 2.5G. It's not as fast as 3G.


LAN (Local Area Network)
If you've got one computer connected to the internet, a LAN lets other computers share the same connection. Most offices use a LAN to share internet, printers and programs.


LOG
A history or record of what you've done on your computer, and when.

Malware
Like a virus, malware is a program that can harm your computer or steal information from it. There are anti-malware programs you can use to protect your computer.


Modem
A piece of kit that lets your computer connect to the internet.


Spyware
Programs which try to steal information from your system. They're a bit like viruses or malware. They often install themselves.


USB (Universal Serial Bus)
A way of plugging things in to your computer – USB cables plug into USB sockets.


Wi-Fi (Wireless Fidelity)
A wireless internet connection.
