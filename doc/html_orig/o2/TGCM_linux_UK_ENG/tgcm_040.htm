Getting online

 

Connecting to Internet

Step 1: Open Connection Manager

By default, Connection Manager will open automatically when you start your system (If you don’t want it to, see  Changing settings section).
If you need to open it yourself:

  • Ubuntu and Fedora:
    • Click Applications, then Internet.
    • Click on the Connection Manager icon.
    • Or click the Connection Manager shortcut on your desktop (if you've got one).

  • OpenSuse:
    • Click Applications, then Internet
    • Click on the Connection Manager icon.
    • Or click the Connection Manager shortcut on your desktop (if you've got one).

     

Step 2: Choose an Internet connection

When you open Connection Manager, it will automatically connect to the internet for you. It will try to connect through the O2 mobile network (if your modem is plugged in).


To choose a different connection:

  • Click on Available Networks button.

  • Click on the connection you want to use and then click on Connect button.
  • Connection Manager will try to join the network. It will ask you for a password, if you need one. When it’s done, you’ll see the network name and the message Status: Connected.

 

Find a O2 Wi-Fi Hotspot

Connection Manager comes with a directory of O2 Wi-Fi Hotspots. Search it to find your nearest hotspot, anywhere in the UK. You can search by town/city, postcode or the type of place (like a coffee shop, airport, hotel and so on).
To find a hotspot:

  1. Click on Find Hotspot icon from dock.
  1. Type in a Location, or choose one from the drop-down lists.

  1. Click Search button.
  1. You’ll see a list of hotspots on the right-hand side of the window. (If there aren’t any, it’ll stay blank).
