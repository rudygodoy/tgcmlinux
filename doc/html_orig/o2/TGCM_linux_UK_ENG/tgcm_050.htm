Extra apps

 

Connection Manager comes with a few extra apps  to help you stay in touch, keep track of your contacts, or find your favourite websites, files and programs quickly

 

Text

To send or read texts from your computer, click Text.


New message

To send a new text:

  • Put the name or number of the person you want to text in the To: box. (If they’re in your Contacts, just highlight their name and double click on it).

To text more than one person at a time, use a comma [,] to separate different names or numbers.

  • Write your text in the Message box.

You can only use 160 characters in one text. Connection Manager will keep count as you type.

  • Click Send.

The sent message will be automatically saved to the Sent folder.

If you are not connected, Connection Manager will hold on to your text and send it as soon as you’re connected again.

Save: If you want to send your message later, click Save. Then, look in your Drafts folder, when you’re ready to finish, edit or send it.

Delete: Click Delete to throw away your text before sending it.

 

 Inbox


You can use O2 Connection Manager to read texts. Click ‘inbox’ to see them. Any unread messages will be in bold.

You can see your texts in a different order: by who sent it (contacts), what it says (content) or when it was sent (date). Click on the one you want.

Then click on any text to read it. It’ll show up in the bottom window.

Reply: If you want to reply a text, highlight and click Reply.

Delete: You can also delete texts by clicking on Delete.

Edit: To edit a text, highlight it and click Edit.

 

 Sent


See all the texts you have sent, from newest to oldest. (Unsent messages are marked as Pending).

Click on any text to read it. It’ll show up in the bottom window.

Delete: To delete a text, highlight it and click Delete.

Edit: To edit a text, highlight it and click Edit.

 

 Chat


In your Chat folder, you can see (from newest to oldest ) all the texts you’ve sent and received from particular people. So it’s easier to follow the trail of your texts.

Show Messages: Tick or untick the Received and Sent box to choose which messages you see.

Contacts: All the people you’ve texted before (in alphabetical order). The number in brackets tells you how many texts you’ve sent each other.

Messages: Choose a contact; all the messages you’ve sent and received will show up in the Messages box. Click on any message to read, reply to, or delete it.

Sent messages will show up in black text on a light background. Received messages will be in white text on a dark background.

[+] and [-]: To read the whole message, click [+]. To see just the subject, click [-]. 

 

 Drafts


This folder holds all the messages you’ve saved, but not sent. Click on any message to read it.

To edit or send a message, click Edit. To delete it without sending it, click Delete.

 

Contacts

This is where you save all your names, phone numbers and e-mail addresses.

  • New: Add a new name, number or email address.
  • Edit: Change the details for one of your contacts.
  • Delete: Remove a contact.
  • Send text: Send a text to someone in your contacts list.
  • Send Email: Send an email to someone in your contacts list.
  • Import: Add contacts from other places (your sim) to Connection Manager.

 

 New


To add a new contact, click New. Type in their name, phone number and email address. Then click Save.


 Edit


To change someone’s details, highlight the contact and click Edit. Make your changes, then click Save.


 Delete


To delete a contact, highlight it then click Delete.


 Send a text


To send a text, highlight the contact first. Then click Send text. Type in your message, then click Send.


 Send a Email


To send an email, highlight the contact first. Then click Send Email. Connection Manager will open a new email (using the email software on your computer). Write and send it as normal.


 Import a contact


Already have contacts in other places (like Outlook Express, Windows Mail, Lotus Notes) or your sim? Use Import to add them to Connection Manager.

  • Click Import
  • Choose where you want to import your contacts from (Device sim).

  • Click Next.

  • Click Finish.


 Search


To find a contact, type a name, number or email address in the search box.

 

Favourites

A quick way of finding and using your Internet address or favorite applications in Connection Manager is to use the option of Favourites. Click on Favourites and you will see all the Favourites you have configured, and you can also add new ones and configure them as well.



  • New: Add a new favourites.
  • Modify: Change de link os shortcut the one of your favourites.
  • Delete: Delete a favourite.
  • Import: Add your favourite websites from browser (like Firefox).
  • Export: Share your Connection Manager favourites with your web browser (like Firefox).

 

 Go to a Favourite place


Double-click any of the favourites on your list to go there.

 

 Add a new Favourite

  1. Click New.
  2. Type in a name (BBC News or Hotmail).
  3. If it’s a website, click URL address and type in the web address; If it’s a file, click File and type in the location (or click Browse to look for it).
  4. To finish, click OK.

 

Change Favourites


To update any details,

  1. Highlight the favourite from the list.
  2. Click Change and make your changes.
  3. Click OK to save your changes.

 

 Delete Favourites


Highlight the favourite you want to delete, then click Delete.

 

 Import Favourites


You can copy favourites from your web browser (like Firefox) into Connection Manager. Here's how:

  1. In your web browser, export your favourites as a file (see your web browser's user guide for help).
  2. In your Connection Manager, click Import.
  3. Find the file on your computer.
  4. Click OK.

 

 Export Favourites


To export your favourites websites from Connection Manager to your web browser (like Firefox):

  1. Highlight the names of the favourites you want to export.
  2. Click Export.
  3. Choose where to save your file.
  4. In your web browser, import the file you've just saved (see your web browser's user guide for help).

 

Find Hotspot

Connection Manager comes with a directory of O2 Wi-Fi Hotspots. Search it to find your nearest hotspot, anywhere in the UK. You can search by town/city, postcode or the type of place (like a coffee shop, airport, hotel and so on).
To find a hotspot:

  1. Click on Find Hotspot icon from dock.
  1. Type in a Location, or choose one from the drop-down lists.

  1. Click Search button.
  1. You’ll see a list of hotspots on the right-hand side of the window. (If there aren’t any, it’ll stay blank).
